Anxiety Disorders:

Generalized Anxiety Disorder

Description:

Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD) can be characterized by exaggerated worry or tension and anxiety that continues to occur even if there is not much of a trigger.

Treatment

  • self-care (find more about this in the "maintaining" page!
  • medication
  • Cognitive behavioral therapy (CBD)

Panic Disorder:

Description:

This disorder is characterized by abrupt and recurring episodes of severe fear, and physical symptoms such as dizziness, chest pain, and shortness of breath.

Treatment

  • psychotherapy
  • medication

Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder:

Description

This anxiety disorder also known as PTSD can be caused by an extremely scary event in which serious injury has happened or was threatened.

Treatment:

  • Trauma focused psychotherapy
  • medication

Social Phobia/Social Anxiety Disorder:

Description

A person who has social phobia has major shyness, or self-consiousness, and anxiety in everyday settings. It's extent can be from just not being able to do one certain thing around people because of anxiety to experiencing symptoms everytime you are around people.

Treatment

  • Cognitive behavioral therapy
  • Psychotherapy
  • Medications (SSRI, Antidepressants and sedatives

Mood Disorders:

Major Depression

Someone who has a depressed mood or loss of interest in activities which they used to before. There are also many behavioral changes such as changes in concentration, daily behavior, self esteem, sleep cycle, apetite and energy level. In extreme cases can also lead to suicidal thoughts

Dysthymia

Dysthimia is a more mild however longer-term version of major depression. This is defined as a low mood lasting for a minimum of two years along with at least two other symptoms of major depression. Find more information on symptoms of depression above!

Bipolar Disorder

Someone with bipolar disorder expieriences extreme mood swings which can range from extremely depressive lows to manic highs. These mood swings can last from days to months and can even involve suicidal thoughts.
