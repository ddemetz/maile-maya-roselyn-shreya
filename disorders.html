Anxiety Disorders:

Generalized Anxiety Disorder

Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD) can be characterized by exaggerated worry or tension and anxiety that continues to occur even if there is not much of a trigger.

Treatment:

  • self-care (find more about this in Wellness!)
  • medication (antidepressants or anticonvulsants)
  • cognitive behavioral therapy (CBD) which involves more psychological treatments than medical ones

Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder:

This, also known as OCD, involves repeated unwelcome thoughts or behaviors. Behaviors such as cleaning, counting, or checking are done in hopes that the feeling of anxiety associated with this will go away but it only gives momentary relief. Not doing those acts only increases anxiety levels.

Treatments:

  • exposure therapy (gradually being exposed to one's obsessions but not performing the compulsions or behaviors that usually eases distress
  • habit reversal training which includes awareness training and social support

Panic Disorder:

This disorder is characterized by abrupt and recurring episodes of severe fear, and physical symptoms such as dizziness, chest pain, and shortness of breath.

Treatment:

  • psychotherapy
  • medication (antidepressants, SSRIs, TCAs, or benzodiazepines)

Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder:

This anxiety disorder also known as PTSD can be caused by an extremely scary event in which serious injury has happened or was threatened.

Treatment:

  • trauma focused psychotherapy
  • medication (Zoloft, Paxil, Prozac, Effexor)

Social Phobia/Social Anxiety Disorder:

A person who has social phobia has major shyness, or self-consiousness, and anxiety in everyday settings. It's extent can be from just not being able to do one certain thing around people because of anxiety to experiencing symptoms everytime you are around people.

Treatment:

  • cognitive behavioral therapy
  • psychotherapy
  • medications (SSRIs, antidepressants, and sedatives)

Mood Disorders:

Major Depression

Someone who has a depressed mood or a loss of interest in activities which they used to like or do before. There are also many behavioral changes such as changes in concentration, daily behavior, self esteem, sleep cycle, appetite, and energy level. In extreme cases, depression can lead to suicidal thoughts.

Treatment:

  • therapy
  • psychotherapy
  • medications (SSRI, Antidepressants, and Anxiolytics)

Dysthymia

Dysthimia is a more mild however longer-term version of major depression. It is defined as a low mood lasting for a minimum of two years along with at least two other symptoms of major depression. Find more information on symptoms of depression above.

Treatment:

  • Physical exercise
  • Psychotherapy
  • Medications (SSRI, Antidepressants and sedatives)

Bipolar Disorder

Someone with bipolar disorder expieriences extreme mood swings which can range from extremely depressive lows to manic highs. These mood swings can last from days to months and can involve suicidal thoughts.

Treatment:

  • therapy(CBT, family therapy, psychotherapy)
  • medication (antipsychotic, SSRI)
  • hospitalization
